# Direction C experiment result — 2026-07-12

## Outcome

The visual experiment succeeded. For the current isolated prototype, **C3 (video fade with a simultaneous training-ball entry)** was the most suitable of the tested configurations.

The reviewer found the presentation acceptable and lively. In table tennis, a serve is very fast; during the short overlap, a difference between the video's ball position and the training ball's chosen entry position did not create a confusing visual handoff in this experiment.

## Conditions observed

- Source: `real_backspin_001`.
- The video remained visible through the short post-contact tail while fading out.
- The training ball appeared at the same time and moved toward the receiver's side of the screen.
- The training entry could be freely repositioned for visual exploration.

## Interpretation and boundary

This is product-readability evidence only. It does **not** establish geometric, velocity, spin, or trajectory continuity between the real-video ball and the training ball. It also does not calibrate `real_backspin_001`, validate physical truth, approve content variants, or authorize Trainer/mainline integration.

The preferred C3 result should therefore be retained as the current prototype presentation choice, not as a formal UI or physics decision. Any integration into `game4.html`, `match-trainer.html`, shared physics, the formal video library, or physics presets remains a separate red-line discussion.
